HP Envy 13:
The ultimate lightweight and fashionable laptop

The new envy 13 notebook computer is the latest masterpiece of HP creative industry ecosystem. It is specially designed for content creators to help them create freely and show their potential!. Though slim and stylish, these laptops are designed for content creators and high-end users. It's time to see how these latest and updated envy notebooks are doing.

The specifications of HP Envy 13 are as follows:

  • Running Windows 10 system
  • 13.3 "FHD IPS screen, BrightView, micro edge, WLED backlit, multitouch enabled
  • Up to Intel core ™ I7-1065g7 processor
  • Intel iris plus graphics (i7-1065g7 / i5-1035g4) / Intel UHD graphics / NVIDIA geforce mx350
  • Up to 16GB DDR4 RAM
  • Up to 1TB m.2 nvme PCIe SSD
  • Thunderbolt 4 port, two USB type-A ports, a headset / microphone jack and a microSD card reader, fingerprint scanning seamless login function.
  • Support 65W charging
  • 51 wh battery
  • Wi Fi 6 support
  • Natural Silver / Pale
  • 306.5 x 194.64 x 16.9 mm
  • 1.3kg
  • Price from RM4399.00

Design

The HP Envy 13 lacks that attention-grabbing design quirk found with rival laptops, such as the edge-to-edge screen of the Dell XPS 13 or the plentiful colour options of the Surface Laptop 3. If the Envy was a crisp flavour, it would be Ready Salted.

That’s not a problem though, as HP has still adhered to modern conventions by maintaining a skinny bezel and minimalist styling. HP has made a number of tweaks to the previous iteration that make this device smaller; shunting the speakers to the bottom while also fitting both the power button and fingerprint scanner into the keyboard.

The Envy comfortably merits an ultrabook classification, weighing a dainty 1.3kg. That’s not quite as light as the Galaxy Book S or Acer Swift 5, but it’s still incredibly effortless to chuck in a bag and be on your way.

HP has borrowed a trick from Asus by having the bottom of the screen lift the main chassis up off the desk when fully opened. This not only positions the keyboard at a more comfortable angle, but also ensures better airflow to prevent the internals getting too hot.

The all-metal body feels smooth and sturdy, providing a level of class that plastic simply can’t match. In terms of build quality, the HP Envy 13 is just as good as any other ultrabook.


Screen

With narrow bezels on three sides, the Envy 13 has an 80.92% screen-to-body ratio. As is typically the case on HP PCs, that means very narrow bezels on the sides, and a slightly larger one on top. As usual, the chin is pretty large, although that can be good for propping up the screen. It actually works quite well with the hinge propping it up as well.

Perfomance

Results show that the Dell XPS 13 is superior when it comes to multi-core CPU performance and graphical grunt, but the fact that the Envy can remain fiercely competitive despite being significantly cheaper is very impressive.

It also retains a significant performance advantage over the budget champion Honor MagicBook 14, especially in terms of integrated graphics. The Envy 13 uses Intel’s Ice Lake graphics to dabble with casual gaming and entry-level creative work. Keep your expectations low though, as it certainly won’t be able to handle AAA blockbusters or intensive 3D modelling.

The one disappointing element of the HP Envy 13 performance is how loud the fans can get, reaching irritating decibels when running benchmark tests or opening certain applications. It’s probably one of the loudest non-gaming laptops I’ve tested in recent months. You can reduce the fan noise the HP command centre, but at the cost of performance.

While the CPU speeds can not be faltered, the SSD benchmark results were disappointing. With read speeds of 1750 MB/s and write speeds of 1749 MB/s, the Envy isn’t the quickest when it comes to saving and loading data. Storage capacity is at least plentiful, with the base model seeing a 512GB SSD.


Conclusion

The HP Envy 13 (2020) is one of the best value laptops currently available, providing a great performance, keyboard and ultra-portable design despite undercutting the price of the majority of the competition.

HP has also used its commercial expertise to drizzle a few privacy features on top too, including a webcam kill switch and a SureView screen that limits the view for background snoopers. If you’re working with sensitive data or just conscious about security, then the Envy brags advantages over offerings from Dell, Microsoft and Samsung.

There are a few small issues here though, including the super-reflective screen, odd placement of certain keys and loud fans, but nothing significant enough to diminish the fantastic value of this laptop. You can get cheaper laptops such as the Honor MagicBook or more stylish alternatives such as the Dell XPS 13, but the HP Envy 13 (2020) hits the sweet spot in terms of price without compromising on quality.
